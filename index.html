Journey to the Clouds.

Plan your ascent to the Kinnaur valley.

By Flight ✈️

The nearest airports are Shimla Airport (Jubbarhatti) (approx. 245–275 km, 7–9 hrs drive) and Chandigarh Airport (approx. 330–350 km, 10–12 hrs drive).

By Road 🚙

Route 1 (Primary): Shimla-Kinnaur route via NH5. This is the recommended route offering gradual acclimatization.
Route 2 (Spiti Circuit): Manali-Kaza route. Travel from Manali via Kunzum Pass to Kaza and then Kalpa. (Note: Kunzum Pass is closed in winter).

By Train & Bus 🚆🚌

The nearest railway stations are Kalka and Shimla. From there, you can take reliable HRTC buses or private buses to Reckong Peo. From Reckong Peo, local buses and taxis easily connect to Kalpa (10 km).

Kinnaur Kailash Range

The primary attraction of Kalpa is the breathtaking, panoramic view of the Kinnaur Kailash range. Watching the sunrise or sunset, when the golden light hits the sacred Shivling-shaped peak, is considered one of the best experiences in the village. It is a deeply spiritual site for both Hindus and Buddhists.

Suicide Point

Located a few kilometers from Kalpa on the way to Roghi village, this spot is famous for its dramatic, steep cliffs and expansive views of the Sutlej River Valley. Despite its grim name, it is a highly popular viewpoint and a spectacular location for photography and experiencing the sheer scale of the Himalayas.

Roghi Village

Famous for its traditional Kinnauri houses and local way of life, the drive to Roghi village is as scenic as the destination itself. It offers deep insights into the heritage, culture, and daily life of the Kinnaur region, all surrounded by beautiful, lush apple orchards.

Narayan-Nagini Temple

A masterpiece of traditional Kinnauri architecture, this temple features intricate wooden carvings and a pagoda-style roof. It reflects the region's spiritual diversity, seamlessly blending Hindu and local mythologies. It is a must-visit for anyone interested in history and craftsmanship.
